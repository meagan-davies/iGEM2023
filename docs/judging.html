Best Software

Our software, "LINKED," merits the award for best software due to its role in addressing a significant challenge in synthetic biology - the selection of suitable linkers for fusion proteins. "LINKED" combines linker data with properties like flexibility and hydrophobicity, streamlining the linker design process and making it more predictive. This directly aligns with iGEM's goal of improving biological circuit design.

The project encourages collaboration and community engagement by enabling contributions of linker sequences through an intuitive interface, fostering an environment where researchers and future iGEM teams can actively participate in knowledge-sharing within the synthetic biology community. This collaborative approach aligns with iGEM's emphasis on enhancing communication and information exchange among its members, making "LINKED" a valuable tool for researchers.

Moreover, "LINKED" integrates advanced tools like DynaMine for flexibility assessment, providing users with rapid and accurate insights into linker characteristics. Additionally, the strategic use of Docker for deployment portability across various environments, streamlining the development and usage of the application.

In conclusion, "LINKED" not only addresses a crucial challenge in synthetic biology but also fosters collaboration and knowledge-sharing while demonstrating technical excellence.

Best Integrated Human Practices

Our team assessed our community's needs by collaborating with a diverse group of stakeholders, including academics, experts from various organisations, and industry leaders to devise an effective solution for high cholesterol treatment. To deepen our understanding of the various components of our project and to explore optimization strategies we sought out and engaged with professors. Simultaneously, conversations with industry leaders shed light on the commercialization process. Additionally, we actively engaged with our community through surveys and interviews with various stakeholders. To address the social determinants of cardiovascular health our team also volunteered with nonprofit organisations. Overall, this inclusive approach incorporated the values, needs, and feedback of all stakeholders, fostering a holistic and community-centred solution.
